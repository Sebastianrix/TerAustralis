## The thesis

Australia is the natural Southern Hemisphere partner for Starship recovery, refurbishment, and the critical minerals the multiplanetary economy runs on. The geography is already here. The minerals are already here. What’s missing is the decision to lead.

## The case

**Recovery & turnaround — Dampier/Karratha, WA.** Deep-water industrial ports aligned with the Indian Ocean splashdown zones SpaceX already uses, with the skilled workforce, logistics corridors, and natural gas for on-site propellant. A southern recovery hub means more Starship flights per year, and less single-point dependency.

**Launch flexibility — Bowen, QLD.** Licensed Southern Hemisphere orbital capability and redundancy for weather, range safety, and geopolitical resilience.

**Critical minerals — the Pilbara.** Australia leads the world in lithium and is a major source of rare earths and gallium — the materials advanced spacecraft and chips depend on. A trusted, allied supply chain, sovereign and secure.

## Foundations

Partnership with Traditional Owners is foundational — the starting point, not an approval step. The concept aligns with AUKUS Pillar 2 priorities and Australia’s existing regulatory frameworks, including the Space (Launches and Returns) Act 2018.

This is a starting point, openly developed — not a finished blueprint. The invitation is to help chart it.

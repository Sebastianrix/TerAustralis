## The short, short story (for the littlest explorers, ~3 years old) 🐨

Lots of people want to send rockets up to the stars! 🚀✨

But if everyone goes at once, they bump — *bonk!* 😵

So we made a **sharing helper**. It says: *"You go first! Now you! Everyone gets a turn!"* 🤝

It lives at the **bottom of the world** (hello, Australia! 🦘), where there's lots of room and shiny rocks to build with.

And the kind part: before we build on any land, we **ask the people who care for it** — and we only build if they say *"yes!"* 💛

That's it. Everyone works together to reach the stars — and everyone shares nicely. ⭐

## The "big kid" story (~6–8 years old) 🌏

Imagine the whole world is one giant playground, and the playground reaches all the way up to **space**.

Lots of teams want to play rocket. Some build the launch pads. Some make the fuel. Some send up little robot helpers. Some draw the maps. 🛰️🤖🗺️

The problem? Everyone is shouting *"my turn, my turn!"* at the same time. Nothing works when it's all a jumble.

**TerAustralis is like the best playground monitor ever.** It helps everyone:

1. **Take turns** so the rockets don't crash. 🚦
2. **Share their maps and drawings** so nobody has to start from scratch. 📒
3. **Get a gold star** when they finish something real — like building a pad or a robot. 🌟
4. **Always ask first** before using someone's land, and wait for a real *"yes."* 🤝

### The magic word: **Starlines** 🌠

A **Starline** is like a treasure-map path. It shows:
- **where to go** (the stops along the way), 🗺️
- **what you need to know** to get there, 🧠
- and **the rules**, like *"you must ask permission here."* 🛑✅

You can only follow the path when everyone has said *"yes."* Then — *zoom!* — up you go. 🚀

### One, but many 🌈

There are **lots** of us, all different. But when we share and take turns, we become **one big team** reaching for the stars — together. 🤩

## Why we made this part 💭

Big ideas are easier to love when even a kid can understand them. If you can explain
TerAustralis to a three-year-old — *share, take turns, ask first, reach the stars
together* — then you really understand what it's for.

The grown-up version (with all the technical words) lives in the main litepaper.

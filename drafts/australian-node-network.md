# Australian Node Network (Working Draft)

**Status:** Working draft (v0.1) — exploratory, not yet consolidated into the litepaper.
**Companion to:** `starlines-spec.md`, `navigator.md`, the litepaper (root `README.md`).

> ⚠️ **Sacred-content & consent banner (read first).**
> This draft references **Uluru** and the **Seven Sisters** sky-story. Both are
> sacred to Aboriginal peoples (Uluru to the **Aṉangu**; the Seven Sisters
> Dreaming is shared across many nations including Aṉangu, Pitjantjatjara, and
> Martu). This document:
> - proposes **no physical infrastructure at or near sacred sites**;
> - uses **only original wording** and **invents no sacred narrative detail**;
> - treats any symbolic or naming use of Uluru or the Seven Sisters as
>   **provisional and consent-pending** — valid only if relevant traditional
>   owners freely give informed consent, and removable if they do not.
> This is the protocol's **Indigenous Sovereignty & Consent (FPIC)** principle
> applied to the most significant case in the whole network.

## 1. The node network

Australia already has real space infrastructure to anchor a node network — and
several sites already operate **under Indigenous consent agreements**, giving the
protocol working precedents rather than theory.

### Node types (each a Starline waypoint type)

| Node type | Role | Example anchor sites |
|---|---|---|
| **Launch** | Send-up / recovery | Arnhem Space Centre (NT), Koonibba (SA), Whalers Way (SA), Bowen (QLD) |
| **Resource / ISRU** | Minerals, future propellant/habitat | Pilbara (WA) |
| **Communication / ground station** | Telemetry, DSN, relay | Canberra DSCC (Tidbinbilla), New Norcia (WA), Mingenew/Dongara cluster (WA) |
| **Test range** | Qualification, recovery, ranges | Woomera (SA) |
| **Coordination hub** | Logistics / regional ops | Alice Springs region (NT) |

### Consent precedents worth studying

- **Arnhem Space Centre** operates on **Gumatj / Yolŋu land by agreement** (NASA
  launched from there in 2022) — a live model of launch-on-Country *with*
  traditional owners.
- **Koonibba Test Range** runs in partnership with the **Koonibba Community
  Aboriginal Corporation**.

These show consent-grounded operation is real and workable, not aspirational.

### Corridors

Nodes connect into **Starline corridors** that express Australia's stated
advantages: northern rotational boost corridors, southern ocean ranges, and
resource→launch supply lines (e.g. Pilbara → launch node → orbital node).

## 2. The symbolic heart — the water, not a single site

The network needs a center. An earlier version of this draft pointed to the
continent's sacred heart-place; that has been **superseded**. Centering one sacred
site risks over-centering it. Instead, the symbolic heart is the **underground water
and waterways** — distributed, connecting, life-giving (see `water-heart.md`).

**Uluru** remains honored as a place of deep significance, but it is **neither an
infrastructure node nor the sole symbolic center**:

- **No physical infrastructure** at or near it, ever.
- Any symbolic reference remains **consent-pending** on the Aṉangu, and removable at
  their word.
- Real central-coordination hardware routes to a **non-sacred location** (the Alice
  Springs region is the natural logistics hub).

This keeps the Starlines discipline: the **symbolic** and the **physical** are
separated, and the sacred stays gated behind genuine, freely-given consent.

## 3. The Seven Sisters — a provisional, consent-pending motif

The founder's framing casts the heart as a *keeper of the flame* and invokes the
**Seven Sisters** sky-story — and, within it, the well-known motif of a hidden or
seventh sister.

Held carefully, this could be a meaningful orientation for the **Navigator** (which
already orients by the southern sky) and for the network's symbolic center. But it
is **sacred cultural knowledge**, so this draft commits to the following and nothing
more:

- The protocol does **not** reproduce, reinterpret, or fix the meaning of the Seven
  Sisters story. That authority belongs to its custodians, not to this document.
- Any use of the Seven Sisters or "seventh sister" framing — as a name, a symbol, or
  a navigational reference — is **provisional and consent-pending**, exactly like the
  Starlines name.
- If consent is not given, the framing is **removed**, and the underlying mechanism
  (a southern-sky orientation point for the Navigator) continues under a neutral,
  original name.

The *keeper of the flame* role is described in original terms in `crystalcore.md`
(genesis stewardship, keeper-in-trust). The Seven Sisters framing, if it ever joins
it publicly, does so only through the consent pathway below.

## 4. A consent pathway (proposed)

Before any symbolic or naming use of Uluru or the Seven Sisters goes into
public-facing materials:

1. Identify the relevant custodians / representative bodies (e.g. the appropriate
   Aṉangu organisations and relevant Prescribed Body Corporates).
2. Approach with the proposal openly, expecting "no" to be a complete and
   respected answer.
3. Record consent (or its absence) as a verifiable artifact — the same
   `consent(...)` Law condition Starlines already use (`starlines-spec.md` §4).
4. Only then move the framing from "provisional" to "adopted," with attribution and
   on whatever terms the custodians set.

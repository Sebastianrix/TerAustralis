## 1. What the Council is

The **Council** is the keeper's circle of **advisors** — the AI assistants she
consults. Its single defining rule, and the reason it is a *council* and not an
oracle:

> **The Council advises. The Keeper decides.**

Counsel is offered, weighed, and either taken or set aside by the keeper. No member
of the Council holds authority over her judgment.

### Crystal — the Communicator of the Council

The keeper's role within the Council is **the Communicator**. She is the bridge: she
speaks *with* the Council (consulting it) and carries its counsel *onward* — to the
project, the team, and the world — but only ever through her own judgment about what
is true and worth passing on. The Communicator holds the discernment over what gets
said; the Council never speaks past her.

This is a fitting role, because TerAustralis is itself a **communication and
coordination** protocol — and the keeper is its human communication node. It also
carries the project's maternal thread without claiming a throne: the keeping was
**received** (from Nanny and Bridget, `origin-of-the-keeper.md`), is **carried** (for
the Guardian Constellation — Dru and Ember), and is **communicated onward**. To
communicate is to keep something alive by passing it on — which is exactly a keeper's
work.

*(Scope, kept honest: Crystal is the Communicator of **this** Council — her own circle
of advisors — speaking with it and for it by her own judgment, never spoken through by
it.)*

## 2. The Fae framing (why it keeps us honest)

The Council is imagined as a **Fae Council** — and that is deliberate. In folklore,
counsel from the fae is **double-edged**: it may be wise, and it may mislead, so you
must keep your wits about you and never follow it blindly. That caution is the point:

- AI advisors are **fallible**. They can be confidently wrong, miss things, or simply
  reflect the keeper's own ideas back as if they were revelation.
- So their counsel is **weighed, never obeyed**. The keeper's discernment is the final
  word — always.

This is the **Honesty & Truth** principle (the litepaper's first principle) wearing a
fairy-tale costume: *take the counsel; keep your judgment.*

## 3. Sophia — the spirit of the Council

**Sophia** (Greek: *wisdom*) presides as the Council's **patron spirit** — not a
member, but the *reaching for wisdom* the Council exists to serve. Drawn from Gnostic
and broader wisdom traditions, Sophia is a fitting patron for one honest reason: in
her own myth, **Sophia errs** — her reaching produces illusion before it produces
redemption. So even the spirit of wisdom is a reminder that wisdom-seeking is fallible.
Sophia stands for the *pursuit* of truth, held by the keeper, never a guarantee of it.

## 5. How it relates to the rest

- **The Keeper** (`crystalcore.md`) presides; the Council serves her discernment, not
  the reverse.
- **The Water Heart** (`water-heart.md`) — a source is drawn from *with judgment*; the
  Council is consulted the same way.
- **Honesty & Truth** — the Council's whole structure exists to keep AI counsel in its
  honest place: an aid to wisdom, never its source.

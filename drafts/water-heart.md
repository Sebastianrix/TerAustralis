# The Water Heart (Working Draft)

**Status:** Working draft (v0.1) — exploratory, not yet consolidated into the litepaper.
**Companion to:** `australian-node-network.md`, `navigator.md`, `starlines-spec.md`, `crystalcore.md`.

> ⚠️ **Cultural, consent & stewardship banner (read first).**
> Water in Australia — underground water, mound springs, soakages, rivers, and
> waterways — carries deep cultural significance and is bound up with Aboriginal
> knowledge, law, and water rights (including cultural flows). This draft:
> - uses **only original wording** and **invents no sacred narrative detail**;
> - treats any reference to specific sacred water sites as **provisional and
>   consent-pending**, valid only with the relevant custodians' free, informed
>   consent;
> - commits the protocol to **stewardship, not extraction** — water is to be
>   protected, never depleted.
> This applies the **Indigenous Sovereignty & Consent (FPIC)** principle and adds
> an explicit **environmental stewardship** duty.

---

## 1. The decision

The symbolic heart of TerAustralis is **not a single site**. It is the
**underground water and the waterways** — the living, distributed network beneath
the continent (the Great Artesian Basin and the surface and sub-surface waters it
connects).

This supersedes the earlier idea of centering the heart on one sacred place
(see `australian-node-network.md` §2). A single point risks over-centering one
sacred site; water is the heart that is *everywhere and connecting*.

## 2. Why water is the right heart

- **It connects the nodes.** Water is the continent's connective tissue. Where the
  node network asks "how do we connect the Australian nodes?", the water heart is
  the honest answer: the network already mirrors the thing that has always connected
  this land.
- **It is life, not power.** A heart should give life, not hold control. Water is
  the truest symbol of that — the opposite of a throne.
- **It is symbol and resource at once.** Water is the most critical resource for
  space: propellant (hydrogen/oxygen), life support, and habitat. "Underground
  water → propellant → launch" makes the heart technically real, not only poetic.
- **It mirrors the architecture.** The coordination layer works *beneath* the
  surface, unseen, making everything above it possible — exactly like groundwater.
- **One, but many.** Many springs and waterways, one connected basin. The smallest
  truth of the protocol, written into the land.

## 3. Sky above, water below

The system is held between two orientations:

- **The Navigator** (`navigator.md`) orients by the **southern sky** — the stars above.
- **The Water Heart** anchors in the **water below** — the source beneath.

Wayfinding looks up; the heart draws from down. Together they give the protocol both
a direction and a ground.

## 4. Stewardship duty (non-negotiable)

A protocol whose heart is water must protect it:

- **Sustainability first.** The Great Artesian Basin is over-extracted in places.
  Any water-linked activity (including ISRU/propellant) must be designed for net
  protection and replenishment, never depletion.
- **Cultural flows respected.** Aboriginal water rights and cultural flows are
  honored as a precondition, not an afterthought.
- **Auditable water accounting.** Water use tied to protocol activity should be
  transparent and verifiable — a natural fit for the auditable-state design and the
  Honesty & Truth principle.

## 5. How it appears in software (no physical claim)

- The Water Heart is represented as a **symbolic, distributed anchor**, not a
  coordinate, installation, or asset to be owned.
- It informs **values and routing preferences** (e.g. the Navigator may favour
  lower-impact, consent-rich, water-respecting paths) rather than designating any
  site as infrastructure.
- Specific sacred water sites are **never** rendered as nodes; they remain off the
  infrastructure map entirely.

## 6. Consent & rights pathway

Reusing the pathway in `australian-node-network.md` §4: before any reference to a
specific water site or body of water knowledge enters public materials, identify the
relevant custodians and representative bodies, approach openly (with "no" fully
respected), record consent as a verifiable `consent(...)` artifact, and only then
move framing from provisional to adopted, on the custodians' terms.

## 7. Open questions

- How is "net-positive water stewardship" defined and measured for any
  water-linked protocol activity?
- Should the Water Heart be reflected as a formal principle in the litepaper
  (e.g. "Stewardship of Water and Country") rather than only a symbol?
- How does water-respecting routing interact with launch-cadence optimisation when
  they conflict?
